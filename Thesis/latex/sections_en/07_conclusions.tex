\section{Conclusions}

This thesis demonstrates that large-scale archived Mie scattering videos of multi-hole diesel injection can be transformed into a calibrated, uncertainty-aware penetration surrogate. The resulting model supports early-stage geometric screening without requiring computational fluid dynamics (CFD) for every candidate.

The main contribution is the workflow rather than the specific neural network backbone. The workflow combines right-censoring-aware target construction, plume-level heteroscedasticity, physically informed amplitude $A$ scaling, raw CDF/teacher knowledge distillation, and a probabilistic wall-impingement interface. Under a unified evaluation protocol, the final multi-stage MLP substantially outperforms two physical baselines and gives more conservative in-distribution uncertainty coverage than the SVGP baseline. At the same time, the SVGP is the stronger point-wise regressor in both the in-distribution and leave-one-nozzle-out evaluations.

The MLP backbone is therefore retained for three structural reasons: its onset-CDF output head, its regime-aware distillation chain, and its low computational cost for the ablation evidence in Chapters 4 and 5. The broader multi-stage surrogate should be understood as a regressor-agnostic instance of the right-censoring-aware learning protocol. The same protocol can host kernel mean functions as well, and would likely benefit from them in out-of-distribution accuracy.

\begin{itemize}
  \item On the conservative uncensored CDF protocol---the primary in-domain point-wise metric---the production three-stage MLP (anchor-off, \texttt{kd\_mse\_mu\_plus\_sigma} loss, \(\lambda_\sigma=5.0\), five seeds 7/17/42/99/2024) reduces RMSE by 58\% and 54\% relative to the calibrated Hiroyasu--Arai and delayed Naber--Siebers baselines (Table~\ref{tab:conclusions-scorecard}). A censoring-inclusive supplementary evaluation on the full cleaned set, which additionally includes the right-censored points, is reported in Chapter~5.
  \item In-distribution, the SVGP is the stronger point-wise regressor, but the MLP is markedly more conservative in uncertainty coverage (1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ \(0.887/0.989\) versus \(0.716/0.945\)); the structural value of the MLP relative to the SVGP therefore lies in its conservative coverage rather than point accuracy.
  \item Under leave-one-nozzle-out transfer the SVGP wins the point RMSE in four of five folds; after excluding the off-design Nozzle0 fold where both architectures collapse (MLP RMSE \(\sim\SI{35}{mm}\), SVGP \(\sim\SI{27}{mm}\)), the 1$\sigma$ coverage gap closes (\(0.696\) versus \(0.697\)). Relative to the physical baseline LONO references (Hiroyasu--Arai \(\SI{10.713}{mm}\pm\SI{1.542}{mm}\), Naber--Siebers \(\SI{9.784}{mm}\pm\SI{2.102}{mm}\)), the picture is split: on the full five-fold mean the Nozzle0 collapse dominates, so the MLP (\(\SI{12.55}{mm}\)) sits above both baselines and the SVGP (\(\SI{10.16}{mm}\)) falls below Hiroyasu--Arai but not Naber--Siebers; only once the off-design Nozzle0 fold is excluded do both architectures fall well below both baselines (MLP \(\SI{7.00}{mm}\), SVGP \(\SI{5.95}{mm}\)). This is precisely the inductive bias characteristic discussed in Section~\ref{sec:kernel-vs-nn-ood-en}; the low coverage of difficult nozzles indicates that the extrapolation risk to true off-design geometries still needs to be explicitly reported.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Current Prototype Boundaries}

\begin{itemize}
  \item The probabilistic wall-interaction screening demonstrates that the penetration surrogate can already be projected into time-resolved geometric risk scores; however, the current rotated Gaussian geometry and toy piston case are merely pipeline demonstrations and have yet to be validated against matching experiments or CFD.
  \item The model should be understood as an early-stage screening surrogate within the non-reacting, room-temperature, Mie-defined operating envelope, rather than a phase-resolved predictor for liquid impingement in a real engine.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Next Development Priorities}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Validate $P_{\mathrm{hit}}(t)$ and cumulative exposure $E$ using matching optical experiments or CFD impingement data.
  \item Replace the current analytical constant-cone-angle envelope with a learnable two-dimensional envelope model.
  \item Quantify seed-to-seed reproducibility and calibration stability across multiple training repetitions.
  \item Introduce nozzle-conditioned transient priors so that difficult cases such as HZ\_Nozzle3 and Nozzle0 can be modeled more explicitly.
\end{enumerate}
