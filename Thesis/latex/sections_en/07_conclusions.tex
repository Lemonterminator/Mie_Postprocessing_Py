\section{Conclusions}

This thesis demonstrates that large-scale archived Mie scattering videos of multi-hole diesel injection can be transformed into a calibrated, uncertainty-aware penetration surrogate, thereby supporting early-stage geometric screening without the need for computational fluid dynamics (CFD). The contributions of this work are the right-censored-aware learning protocol---limited field of view handling, plume-level heteroscedasticity, physically informed amplitude $A$ scaling, and raw CDF/teacher knowledge distillation---as well as the probabilistic wall-impingement interface, rather than the specific neural network backbone hosting it. The final multi-stage MLP significantly outperforms two physical baselines under a unified evaluation protocol, and is notably more conservative in in-distribution uncertainty coverage than the sparse heteroscedastic Gaussian process baseline. However, SVGP is a stronger regressor in terms of point-wise accuracy for both in-distribution and leave-one-nozzle-family-out evaluations. Thus, the MLP backbone is retained due to its onset-CDF output head, regime-aware distillation chain, and the ablation affordability upon which the design attribution evidence in Chapters 4 and 5 relies; whereas the multi-stage surrogate should be understood as a regressor-agnostic instantiation of the right-censored-aware learning protocol: the same protocol can equally host kernel mean functions, and would likely benefit from it in out-of-distribution accuracy.

\subsection{Validated Results}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Under the refreshed fully-cleaned CDF evaluation protocol (692{,}942 prediction points, 24{,}316 trajectories, five training repetitions with seeds 7/17/42/99/2024), the production three-stage MLP (anchor-off, \texttt{kd\_mse\_mu\_plus\_sigma} loss, \(\lambda_\sigma=5.0\)) achieves a mean RMSE of \(\SI{4.735}{mm}\pm\SI{0.037}{mm}\), MAE of \(\SI{3.293}{mm}\pm\SI{0.039}{mm}\), bias of \(\SI{+0.626}{mm}\pm\SI{0.112}{mm}\), P95 of \SI{9.167}{mm}, and conservative 1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ coverages of 0.867/0.985.
  \item Restricting the same five-repetition evaluation to the conservative uncensored CDF subset (leaving 542{,}565 points after truncating density drops and FOV saturation) yields an RMSE of \(\SI{4.265}{mm}\pm\SI{0.040}{mm}\), MAE of \(\SI{3.032}{mm}\pm\SI{0.048}{mm}\), and 1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ coverages of 0.887/0.989, demonstrating that the primary conclusions are not contingent upon the presence of right-censored points.
  \item Relative to the calibrated Hiroyasu--Arai (RMSE \SI{11.268}{mm}) and delayed Naber--Siebers (RMSE \SI{10.545}{mm}) baselines under the same protocol, the three-stage MLP reduces RMSE by 58.0\% and 55.1\%, respectively. The sparse heteroscedastic Gaussian process is a stronger point-wise regressor (RMSE \SI{4.628}{mm}), but its 1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ coverages are only \(0.703/0.939\), compared to the MLP's \(0.867/0.985\); on the uncensored CDF subset, the MLP's coverage advantage further expands to \(0.887/0.989\) versus \(0.716/0.945\). The structural value of the MLP relative to SVGP in-distribution therefore lies in its conservative coverage rather than point accuracy.
  \item The leave-one-nozzle-family-out evaluation employs the same uncensored CDF point set as the MLP LONO ablation; the MLP five-fold mean RMSE is \SI{12.55}{mm}, while the SVGP is \SI{10.16}{mm}. After excluding the off-design Nozzle0 fold where both architectures collapse (MLP RMSE \(\sim\SI{35}{mm}\), SVGP \(\sim\SI{27}{mm}\)), the MLP four-fold mean narrows to \SIrange{7.0}{7.2}{mm}, and the SVGP to \SI{5.95}{mm}. Both architectures are significantly lower than the physical baseline LONO reference values (Hiroyasu--Arai \(\SI{10.713}{mm}\pm\SI{1.542}{mm}\), Naber--Siebers \(\SI{9.784}{mm}\pm\SI{2.102}{mm}\)), but the SVGP wins the point RMSE in four out of five folds, and the 1$\sigma$ coverage gap has closed for the four folds (\(0.697\) versus \(0.696\)). This is precisely the inductive bias characteristic discussed in Section~\ref{sec:kernel-vs-nn-ood-en}; the low coverage of difficult nozzle families indicates that the extrapolation risk to true off-design geometries still needs to be explicitly reported.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Current Prototype Boundaries}

\begin{itemize}
  \item The probabilistic wall-interaction screening demonstrates that the penetration surrogate can already be projected into time-resolved geometric risk scores; however, the current rotated Gaussian geometry and toy piston case are merely pipeline demonstrations and have yet to be validated against matching experiments or CFD.
  \item The model should be understood as an early-stage screening surrogate within the non-reacting, room-temperature, Mie-defined operating envelope, rather than a phase-resolved predictor for liquid impingement in a real engine.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Next Development Priorities}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Validate $P_{\mathrm{hit}}(t)$ and cumulative exposure $E$ using matching optical experiments or CFD impingement data.
  \item Replace the current analytical constant-cone-angle envelope with a learnable two-dimensional envelope model.
  \item Quantify seed-to-seed reproducibility and calibration stability across multiple training repetitions.
  \item Introduce nozzle-family-conditioned transient priors so that difficult cases such as HZ\_Nozzle3 and Nozzle0 can be modeled more explicitly.
\end{enumerate}
