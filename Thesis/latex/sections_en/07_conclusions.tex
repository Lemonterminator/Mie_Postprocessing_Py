\section{Conclusions}

This thesis demonstrates that archive-scale Mie-scattering videos of multi-hole diesel injection can be turned into a calibrated, uncertainty-aware penetration surrogate capable of early-stage geometric screening without CFD. The contribution is the censoring-aware learning protocol --- finite-field-of-view treatment, plume-level heteroscedasticity, the physically motivated $A$ amplitude scaling, and raw-CDF/teacher distillation --- together with the probabilistic wall-impingement interface, not the specific neural backbone that realises it. On the shared evaluation protocol the resulting staged MLP outperforms both physical baselines by a wide margin and remains deliberately more conservative in in-distribution uncertainty coverage than the sparse heteroscedastic Gaussian-process alternative backbone, but the SVGP is the stronger pointwise regressor on both in-distribution and the leave-one-nozzle-out evaluation. The MLP backbone is therefore retained for its onset-CDF head, its regime-aware distillation chain, and the ablation affordability that supports the design-attribution evidence in Chapters~4 and~5, while the staged surrogate is understood as a regressor-agnostic instantiation of the censoring-aware learning protocol: the same protocol can host a kernel mean function and likely benefit from it on out-of-distribution accuracy.

\subsection{Validated outcomes}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Under the refreshed full-clean CDF evaluation protocol, covering 692{,}942 prediction points and 24{,}316 trajectories across five repeated runs (seeds 7/17/42/99/2024), the production three-stage MLP (anchor-off, \texttt{kd\_mse\_mu\_plus\_sigma} loss with \(\lambda_\sigma=5.0\)) reaches a mean RMSE of \(\SI{4.735}{mm}\pm\SI{0.037}{mm}\), MAE of \(\SI{3.293}{mm}\pm\SI{0.039}{mm}\), bias of \(\SI{+0.626}{mm}\pm\SI{0.112}{mm}\), P95 absolute error of \SI{9.167}{mm}, and conservative 1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ coverage of 0.867 / 0.985.
  \item Restricting the same five-seed evaluation to the conservatively uncensored CDF subset (542{,}565 points after density-drop and FOV-saturation truncation) yields RMSE \(\SI{4.265}{mm}\pm\SI{0.040}{mm}\), MAE \(\SI{3.032}{mm}\pm\SI{0.048}{mm}\), and 1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ coverage of 0.887 / 0.989, confirming that the headline result is not driven by right-censored points.
  \item Relative to the calibrated Hiroyasu--Arai (RMSE \SI{11.268}{mm}) and delayed Naber--Siebers (RMSE \SI{10.545}{mm}) baselines on the same protocol, the three-stage MLP reduces RMSE by 58.0\% and 55.1\%, respectively. The sparse heteroscedastic Gaussian process is the stronger pointwise regressor (RMSE \SI{4.628}{mm}) but reaches only \(0.703/0.939\) 1$\sigma$/2$\sigma$ coverage against the MLP's \(0.867/0.985\); on the uncensored CDF subset the MLP coverage advantage widens to \(0.887/0.989\) vs.\ \(0.716/0.945\). The MLP's structural value over the SVGP at in-distribution is therefore conservative-coverage rather than point accuracy.
  \item The leave-one-nozzle-family evaluation, under the same uncensored-CDF point set used for the MLP LONO ablation, gives a five-fold mean RMSE of \SI{12.55}{mm} for the MLP versus \SI{10.16}{mm} for the SVGP; excluding the out-of-design Nozzle0 fold (on which both architectures collapse, MLP RMSE \(\sim\SI{35}{mm}\), SVGP \(\sim\SI{27}{mm}\)) the four-fold means become \(\SIrange{7.0}{7.2}{mm}\) for the MLP and \(\SI{5.95}{mm}\) for the SVGP. Both architectures remain well below the physical-baseline LONO references (Hiroyasu--Arai \(\SI{10.713}{mm}\pm\SI{1.542}{mm}\), Naber--Siebers \(\SI{9.784}{mm}\pm\SI{2.102}{mm}\)), but the SVGP wins point RMSE on four of five folds and the four-fold 1$\sigma$ coverage gap is closed (\(0.697\) vs.\ \(0.696\)). This is the expected inductive-bias signature discussed in Section~\ref{sec:kernel-vs-nn-ood-en}; under-coverage on the hardest families indicates that extrapolation risk to genuinely out-of-design nozzle geometries must still be reported explicitly.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Current prototype boundary}

\begin{itemize}
  \item The probabilistic wall-interaction screen demonstrates that the penetration surrogate can be projected into a time-resolved geometric risk score, but the current rotated-Gaussian geometry and toy piston case are only a pipeline demonstration and remain unvalidated against matching experiments or CFD.
  \item The model should be interpreted as an early-stage screening surrogate within a non-reacting, room-temperature, Mie-defined operating domain, not as a phase-resolved predictor of real-engine liquid-wall impingement.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Next development priorities}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Validate $P_{\mathrm{hit}}(t)$ and cumulative exposure $E$ against matching optical or CFD collision data.
  \item Replace the analytic constant-angle envelope with a learned two-dimensional envelope model.
  \item Quantify seed-to-seed reproducibility and calibration stability across repeated training runs.
  \item Extend the surrogate with nozzle-family-conditioned transient priors so that hard cases such as HZ\_Nozzle3 and Nozzle0 are treated more explicitly.
\end{enumerate}
