\begin{frame}{Why This Version Replaces the April Deck}
\small
\begin{block}{Key change}
The May pipeline uses cleaner and larger upstream CDF/clean data, and the feature
engineering changed from \(\Delta P^{0.25}\) to the data-supported
\(\Delta P^{0.5}\) scale. Therefore, the 18 April tuned run is no longer the
current best model, and it should not be compared directly to the retrained model
as a percentage improvement.
\end{block}

\begin{block}{Strict reporting convention in this deck}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Headline results use the common external clean diagnostic:
  \num{795561} points and \num{33845} trajectories.
  \item The main result is the five-seed aggregate of the \(\Delta P^{0.5}\)
  Stage-3 pipeline.
  \item Diagnostic plots use representative best single seed 99; these plots show
  error structure but do not replace the five-seed conclusion.
  \item The 2026-04-18 figures are archival and are not used for current model-comparison claims.
\end{itemize}
\end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Metric Contract}
\small
\begin{tabular}{L{0.23\linewidth}L{0.67\linewidth}}
\toprule
Metric & Meaning \\
\midrule
RMSE & \(\sqrt{\frac{1}{n}\sum_i(\hat y_i-y_i)^2}\); tail-sensitive primary accuracy metric. \\
MAE & \(\frac{1}{n}\sum_i|\hat y_i-y_i|\); typical absolute millimetre error. \\
Bias & \(\frac{1}{n}\sum_i(\hat y_i-y_i)\); signed systematic over-/under-prediction. \\
P95 abs. err. & 95th percentile of \(|\hat y-y|\); tail-risk metric. \\
Coverage & \(\Pr(|y-\mu|\leq k\sigma)\). Calibrated Gaussian targets are 0.683 and 0.954 for 1\(\sigma\)/2\(\sigma\). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.6em}
\footnotesize
This deck therefore reports current absolute error, seed stability, tail error,
and uncertainty coverage under the clean protocol; it no longer states improvement
against the 4/16 baseline.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Conclusions}
\small
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \goodtext{Current reportable best result}: the \(\Delta P^{0.5}\) Stage-3 MLP reaches a five-seed external-clean RMSE of \SI{6.211}{mm} \(\pm\) \SI{0.283}{mm}.
  \item \goodtext{Latest single-seed diagnostic}: seed 99 reaches RMSE \SI{5.948}{mm}, MAE \SI{4.383}{mm}, and bias \SI{-0.074}{mm}, showing that global under-prediction is strongly reduced in the new pipeline.
  \item \notetext{Uncertainty readout}: clean-set 1\(\sigma\)/2\(\sigma\) coverage is 0.774/0.981 for the five-seed mean, so the model is conservative rather than under-covering.
  \item \warntext{Remaining risk}: Nozzle3 still shows a geometry-dependent failure mode; trajectory-level review remains necessary.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{block}{Thesis convention}
Do not compare the May retrained model directly against the April model as a
percentage improvement; they use different clean data scale and a different
feature-scaling protocol.
\end{block}
\end{frame}
