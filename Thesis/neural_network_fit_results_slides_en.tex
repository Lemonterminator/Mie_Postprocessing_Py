\begin{frame}{Conclusions}
\small
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \goodtext{The tuned Stage-3 run is the current best}: RMSE is \SI{6.60}{mm}, MAE is \SI{4.81}{mm}, and NRMSE is 6.88\%.
  \item \goodtext{The main systematic issue is reduced}: global bias improves from \SI{-1.29}{mm} to \SI{-0.40}{mm}; DS300 bias improves from \SI{-3.36}{mm} to \SI{-2.35}{mm}.
  \item \notetext{The ablation supports the KD-dominance hypothesis}: strong KD in deterministic raw-supported bins pushes the student toward teacher bias; lower KD and restored raw supervision improve DS300.
  \item \warntext{The remaining errors are geometry-dependent}: Nozzle3 and some HZ nozzles show a trade-off with DS300, suggesting early-SOI acceleration and curvature priors should be nozzle-aware.
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.5em}
\begin{block}{Thesis-ready message}
The loss tuning is not merely numerical hyperparameter search: it reveals a supervision conflict between raw deterministic observations and teacher distillation, and it exposes nozzle-family-dependent early spray acceleration behavior.
\end{block}
\end{frame}
